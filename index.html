I also expected somewhat of a Hispanic American experience due to the modernization of her name, and use of language. The image that also popped into my mind was white pure sugar, and the fine grain delicacy of it. Experience 1: Quote

One the experience I really enjoyed was the writing of the quote both in English and Spanish on walls, which really connects all the elements of the exhibit. Creating a theme of bittersweet, explaining the feeling of sorrow and remorse the artist is trying to convey.

Experience 2: Photography

The photography gave me a surreal moment as it provided a human perspective to the art, by the artist using burnt sugar to coat trap and engulf individuals gave a new meaning to the impact sugar had on lives.

Experience 3: Statues of Spears with the Sugar

Statues of Spears with the Sugar: Another awe moment I experience, was the visual experience of seeing standing ancient spears with burnt sugar blocks with different shades of brown surrounding it. It portrayed the complex relationship of indigenous culture and the sugar slave trade, inferencing the destruction of culture.

Discovery 1: Artist Race

One shocking discovery, was the race of the artist, I expected her to be of mestizo Cuban background but surprisingly and uniquely she came from African decent. Unknowing of the Afro-Cuban community, I expected the artist to depict the background of European based cuban, not the Afro-Latinos of the island.

Discovery 2: Symbolism of Sugar

After my experience in the gallery, I had a new and complex relationship with sugar, than before. I know look at sugar and imagine the pain and suffering slaves went to produce the pure white substance, often neglecting the corrosive, destructive inpurities that were thrust upon the slaves. Sugar for some might be sweet, but sugar for the artist has a bitter after taste of regret, pain and misery.

Connection 1: Intersectionality

One major connection I made is restructuring my own meaning of Latinidad, or the shared attribute that make up the Latinx community. I made my think about my own basis and closed minded perspective that a Latino can only lock or be from a certain place. This exhibit reminded me that Latinidad encopases many indenities, meaing it will never have a homogeneous, concrete definition, it will forever by dynamic and ever changing.

Connection 2: Spanish to Convey Meaning

Finally another connections that I made with my identity was the use of language and bilingualism to convey different feelings for individuals. The use of Spanish text did create an inclusive environment, but it also paid homage to the culture and people of the island, as the quotes had a deeper meaning to individuals that the misinterpreted words in Spanish. I learned that knowing Spanish create and develop a deeper connections between culture and communities.
